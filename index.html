What is CDAP?

The CDAP, launched by the Government of Canada, assists small and medium-sized enterprises (SMEs) in adopting digital technologies to enhance their competitiveness. Qualified businesses will be granted funds to develop a digital plan and can then apply for up to $100,000 to put that plan into action.

What does it cover?

The program covers items such as:

  • Business funding
  • Inventory Management
  • Free consultation and online marketing
  • Free website or website updates
  • E-Commerce
  • Wage subsidy
  • Acquiring IT equipment
  • Installation of new technologies
  • Updating and/or expanding IT equipment
  • Materials and support services directly related to onboarding the technology
  • Further advisory services on particular aspects of the digital adoption plan
  • Purchasing custom or off the shelf software
  • Updating and/or expanding IT infrastructure
  • Training staff on current and emerging technologies
  • Incorporating communication processes to save time and improve productivity
  • Leveraging digital marketing to reach new markets
  • Gain insights on the latest sales tactics and KPIs
  • Improve data collection and analysis

This is for any business in Canada that makes at least $500,000 in revenue (or sales) in one of the last 3 years and has at least 1 employee that's not an owner.
